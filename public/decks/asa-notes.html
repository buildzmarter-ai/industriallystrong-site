ASA Consistency Checklist

Run this after every revision to the four ASA pages. Last run: 2026-04-14.

1. Canonical definition

Autonomous Structural Arbitration (ASA) is a deterministic replica-arbitration layer for distributed storage systems, selecting a winning replica branch based on structural evidence rather than controller authority.

This exact sentence must appear near the top of all four pages. Never use older labels like "concept," "structural approach," or "distributed correctness engine" without also anchoring to "deterministic replica-arbitration layer."

2. Terminology alignment

Primary label: "deterministic replica-arbitration layer"

Stack position: "above data protection (RAID, mirroring, erasure coding), below SDS / replication control planes"

Competing replica histories: always "branches" (not "version," "timeline," or "fork")

Atomic unit: "event" or "mutation" (not "transaction," "operation," "update" interchangeably)

Deployment: "SDS plug-in or replication-layer module" (primary); "node-local agent" (secondary); "sidecar library" (avoid leading with)

4. Scoring dimensions

Cc Causal coherence · Sc Structural consistency · Ec Event completeness · Oa Operator alignment

Formula: S = w_c*C_c + w_s*S_c + w_e*E_c + w_o*O_a (same letters and order everywhere)

5. Decision outcomes (three classes)

Auto-collapse — ASA chooses a winner on its own (confidence gap ≥ Δ)

Defer to arbitration — ASA preserves branches and raises a ticket

Preserve under concurrency — ASA keeps multiple branches active until more evidence arrives

All four pages must use exactly these three labels. No inventing new outcome terms ("soft resolve," "manual override") — those are instances of "defer to arbitration."

6. Beachhead sentence

ASA's initial beachhead is stretched-cluster split-brain and post-partition recovery, where multiple replicas disagree after a failure and existing quorum-based mechanisms leave ambiguity about which branch is semantically correct.

7. Non-goals (same three bullets everywhere)

ASA does not replace RAID, mirroring, erasure coding, or hardware controllers.

ASA does not enforce consistency via locking or global quorum; it arbitrates between branches after divergence.

ASA does not require application changes for the initial beachhead.
